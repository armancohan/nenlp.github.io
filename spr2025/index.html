New England NLP Meeting Series
April 2025, Yale University, New Haven

About The Meeting

New England Natural Language Processing (NENLP) is a meeting series for academic and industry NLP researchers that live and work in New England.

Call For Papers

We invite submissions for the NENLP 2024 meetup, a one-day event dedicated to exploring the latest developments in NLP research. We welcome submissions on all aspects of NLP. Some of them will be selected for oral presentations and the remaining will be presented as posters. We encourage submissions from rising researchers who are enrolled in graduate programs at universities located in the New England region.

Key Dates

  • Submission deadline: : April 4, 2025, (AOE)
  • Notification: April 9, 2025, (AOE)
  • Event Date (tentative): April 11, 2025

Submission Guidelines

Submissions can be a work in progress or already published work. The overall length of the extended abstract should not exceed 500 words.
Titles and abstracts should be submitted through the link by the submission deadline (Submission link will be available once the event date is confirmed).

Scope

We welcome contributions across a broad spectrum of topics, including but not limited to:
  • Large Language Models: Advances in (large) language models, training techniques, and evaluation methodologies.
  • Multimodality: Integrating language with vision, audio, and other modalities.
  • Low-Resource and Multilingual NLP: Techniques for low-resource settings, underrepresented languages, and data-efficient learning.
  • Human-Centered NLP: Usability, fairness, ethics, and societal impacts of NLP technologies.
  • Math, Logical and Commonsense Reasoning: Enhancing reasoning abilities in NLP systems.
  • NLP for Scientific Discovery: Applications in literature review, hypothesis generation, and research acceleration.
  • Evaluation and Robustness: Developing metrics and ensuring reliability of NLP systems in real-world settings; Development of comprehensive evaluation metrics and benchmarking frameworks; Meta-evaluation techniques to validate and refine evaluation methodologies
  • Safety and Alignment: Research methods to mitigate risks, prevent misuse, and enhance system reliability; Develop frameworks to monitor and improve safety in AI deployment.
  • Interpretability: Mechanistic interpretability and understanding of the inner workings of language models
  • Efficiency: Advances in model compression, efficient training, and distillation techniques
  • Embodied AI: Integrating perception, action, and robotics with language understanding
  • Novel applications: Healthcare, education, finance, law, and other domains.
Accepted papers will be presented as posters, with a subset selected for oral presentations. The workshop will take place in person in New Haven.
